Risks of Using a Collaboration Platform

To streamline communication and foster teamwork, many organisations are relying on collaboration platforms, especially for remote work. These digital solutions support seamless coordination and improve productivity, but are not without risks. Selecting the wrong collaboration platform may not just introduce new problems into your workflow and communications, but it could completely compromise your organisation and clients. Security breaches, data leaks, and the mishandling of sensitive information are just a few potential pitfalls.

Let’s take a closer look at an unfortunate, but sadly not uncommon case. Over 700,000 individuals were exposed when using the Tigo video chat platform. Personal messages, account photos, and email addresses were some of the identifying information stolen in the more than 100 million records leaked. Unfortunately, the company did not protect its clients with proper encryption, nor was it responsive to those raising concerns about possible data breaches.

A more reputable company, T-Mobile, settled for $350 million in a class action lawsuit after 75 million individuals were exposed in a security breach. These incidents cause major harm to both companies and customers, with steep financial costs and long-term reputation damage. According to Fortinet’s Global Study, 62% of businesses surveyed experienced a security breach attributable to working remotely. However, only 37% planned a substantial increase in their budgeting towards security concerns. As we have seen, this lack of prioritisation could result in irreparable damage to one’s reputation and finances.
